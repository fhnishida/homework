The mean of the distribution characterized by the normal CDF is evidently 0. This is also the mean of the distribution characterized by the EDF of the residuals, because the residuals sum to zero. $$$$ The variance of the distribution characterized by the normal CDF is $\sigma^2$. This is not the variance of the EDF of the residuals, which, by definition, is $$\sigma^2_{EDF}\, = \, \sum^n_{t=1} \hat{u}^2_t Pr(Z=\hat{u}_t) \, = \, \sum^n_{t=0} \frac{1}{n} \, = \, \frac{1}{n} \sum^n_{t=1} \hat{u}^2_t .$$ First, note that EDF variance is not equal to Normal CDF variance:

#Comparing variance of distribution of normal CDF variance with variance of EDF
var_EDF = sum(residuals_ecdf^2)/200
sigma_EDF = sqrt(var_EDF)
print(paste("EDF variance =", round(var_EDF, 6), ">", round(sigma^2, 6), "Normal CDF variance"))

If we adjust the residuals by multiplying them by $\sigma/\sigma_{EDF}$, we would have the a pretty close variance as the normal CDF.

In [8]:
#Adjusting EDF variance and comparing with normal CDF variance again
adjusted_EDF_var = var(residuals_ecdf*sigma/sigma_EDF)
print(paste("Adjusted EDF variance =", round(adjusted_EDF_var, 6), "~", round(sigma^2, 6), "Normal CDF variance"))
